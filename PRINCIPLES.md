# MahaJob — Guiding Principles

## 1. Small businesses come first
MahaJob exists to serve small, local businesses — the shop owner hiring one helper, the small hotel needing a cook. Every product decision must be evaluated through this lens. We will never make small companies feel left behind in favor of larger staffing agencies or enterprises.

## 2. Free must be fully useful
The platform must remain completely functional without paying. Free employers can post jobs and receive calls/messages. Free job seekers can browse, search, and contact employers. Premium is an optional advantage, not a paywall that gates core functionality.

## 3. Simplicity over sophistication
Our users are non-technical — small business owners and workers in Maharashtra. Every feature must be simple enough that someone who has never used a job portal can figure it out. If a feature needs explanation, it's too complex. No unnecessary sign-ups, no complex workflows, no jargon.

## 4. Don't build for problems that don't exist yet
Avoid premature complexity. Don't add job caps, tiered pricing, anti-abuse systems, or enterprise features until there's real evidence they're needed. Build the simplest thing, observe, then iterate. Manual intervention (admin tools) is fine at small scale.

## 5. Lower barriers, not raise them
When in doubt, make things easier to access, not harder. Optional accounts over mandatory ones. Simple passwords over complex requirements. Phone numbers over email verification. Remove friction wherever possible.

## 7. Marathi-first, mobile-first
The primary audience uses Android phones and speaks Marathi. Every page, every button, every message should work in Marathi on a small screen first. English is a secondary option, desktop is a secondary layout.

## 8. Direct connections over complex workflows
The core value of MahaJob is connecting employers and job seekers directly — a phone call, a WhatsApp message. Don't replace this with application forms, chat systems, or multi-step hiring pipelines. Enhance the direct connection, don't abstract it away.

## 9. Don't be a middleman eyeing a commission
MahaJob is a platform, not a broker. We don't take a cut from hires, don't charge job seekers, and don't insert ourselves between employer and candidate. Revenue comes from optional tools that help employers find candidates faster — not from taxing the connection itself.

## 10. UX is sacred — don't sell it out
Never degrade the user experience to squeeze in ads or promotions. No intrusive banners, no pop-ups blocking content, no ads disguised as job listings. The platform should feel clean and trustworthy, not like a billboard.

## 11. Respect the user — never spam, never push
Never spam users. Never sell them something they don't need. Never use dark patterns, fake urgency, or misleading CTAs.Never sell user data. Don't shove premium upsells or notifications down anyone's throat — especially job seekers, who are often in a vulnerable position looking for work. Every interaction should add value. If something isn't genuinely helpful to the user, don't show it.

## 12. No gimmicky features
If it doesn't solve a real problem, don't build it. No vanity features, no "cool but useless" additions, no features added just to look competitive. Every feature must earn its place by genuinely helping someone find a job or hire a candidate.
